Abstract —Wild Deer’s grazing in farmers and villager’s ﬁeld
has caused serious troubles for the residents and concerned
authorities here in Northern Hokkaido of Japan. Furthermore,
the number of deer in northern Japan is increasing rapidly
leading to increment in road accidents which is also becoming
a serious problem that needs to be handled. Several procedures
have been followed to avoid or at least minimize the destruction
caused by the white-tailed deer, but a solid solution is yet to
be found. To this context, we are proposing a highly effective
solutions to identify the individual wild deer and pinpoint the
most trouble causing group of deer’s using augmented triplet
network, a novel technique of computer vision. Initially the deer
are recognized and classiﬁed individually which will help us track
the deer and know the behavior of the deer in depth. Our research
method identiﬁes individual deer using several steps where we
initially take video feed which is further divided into several
frames. The extracted frames are then cleaned and processed for
the training efﬁciency. The cleaned frames are then sent to the
Convolution Network where the Nested triplet loss is calculated
for the maximum accuracy.
Index T erms —Triplet Loss, Augmented Triplet Network,
Unique Individual Detection, Metric Learning
I. I NTRODUCTION
The number of Ezo Sika deer ( Cervus nippon yesoensis )
in Japan has been increasing rapidly. The damage caused by
Ezo Sika deer ranges from collisions with deer, impact on
vegetation, and damage to agriculture and forestry. In order to
prevent such kinds of damage, it is necessary to manage not
only the number of deer’s in a systematic manner but also to
conduct population surveys and monitoring scientiﬁcally. The
indices are used to calculate the number of individuals applied
aerial surveys, visual counts by hunters, number of captures,
and number of sightings. The objectives of this study are of
two folds: (1) to investigate the behavior of the same individual
(group) and ʢ2) to automate the counting of individuals. Weadopted the triplet loss model, which is one of the machine
learning models, to identify the same individual.
Then, we propose the Augmented Triplet loss(A TL) model
in section III. A TL is a model that learns to clearly distinguish
between groups that are close in distance in embedded space.
Fig. 1 shows the overall picture of this research. Firstly, we
explain the data-processing part. We have used the Raspberry
Pi and cameras. The server will be crowded with masses of
images and will be less efﬁcient. Thus to minimize the data
overload in the server, the Raspberry Pi is programmed to
send only the feed while the camera captures a deer during
the surveillance. Secondly, we collected video images of Ezo
Sika deer from a ﬁxed-point camera set up outdoors. Then, we
automatically extract frames from the video images and label
them. We classiﬁed the image dataset into training data and
test data. Then, from the obtained training data, we train CNNs
and train classiﬁcations using the Triplet loss function. After a
certain number of Mis exceeded, the proposed loss function
called Augmented Triplet loss(A TL) is used for training. The
details of A TL are explained in Section III. A TL is designed
to reduce the learning time and memory cost of the existing
triplet loss. In this study, we classiﬁed images of Ezo Sika deer,
but it can be applied to various types of image classiﬁcation.

A. Triplet Network
Triplet neural network is a way to train neural networks
that do not require special architecture and allow us to takecomplex data and project it onto a feature space so wecan identify similarities between different data points. Tripletnetwork [13] is an improvement of the Siamese network [14].In a Triplet network, three input sample images are needed
the three samples are represented by anchor sample, anchor
positive, and anchor negative. If we denote the three sampleinputs as x
a,xp,xnthe positive distance is calculated as
dp=d(f(xa),f(xp))and negative anchor is calculated as
dn=d(f(xa),f(xn)). Before applying the triple network,
we pick an anchor sample then pick the positive sample fromthe same category whereas the negative sample is picked froma different category. The triplet loss for the triplet network isdeﬁned as L
triplet =[dp−dn+m] wheremis the margin
which is the desired distance between the positive anchor andthe negative anchor.

C. Implementation
Python Package Pytorch [15] is used for the training and
testing of our dataset. For the learning process in our model,
we are referencing the Pytorch metric learning [16] to sepa-
rate the classes which are then further improvised using the
augmented triplet learning. The augmented triplet method of
ours searches the center point of the class.
The Center of a class is used to group the class together
resulting in the clear separation of the similar class from
the different class. Several key functions like losses, miners
(mining function), and reducers (loss reducing function) are
considered and efﬁciently utilized when training the data. The
distance between the anchors is calculated on the basis of
cosine similarity. The triplet losses which are greater than
0.1 are reduced by the Threshold Reducer. Triplet miners
will output the tuple of size 3 which includes sample anchor,
positive anchor, and the negative anchor. The batch size is
set to 4 letting the training to be done on the chunks of4. The batch is trained in a total of 10 epochs. Our model
contains two convolutional layers with a 3 and 6 channel
input each convolution layer is followed by the max-pooling
of(2,2). We have used the stride of 2 to deal with the
variation of the dataset. The mining function takes the margin
of 0.2 where the margin is the difference between the anchor-
positive distance and anchor-negative distance. The pairs are
selected based on the margin. All the anchors which violate
the margin are considered triplets. The class mining part is
used to generate pairs of classes that violate (1). The dummy
anchors are generated by calculating the center of gravity of
each of the obtained class pairs. In the Augmented Mining
part, from the obtained dummy anchors di, we generate as
many pairs as the sum of the size of the class Ciand the
size ofCjas|Ci|+|Cj|. In the Augmented Loss calculation
part, the dummy anchors are used to calculate the pairs that
violate the (2), (3). Afterward, optimization is performed and
the learning is evaluated.

IV . R ESULT AND DISCUSSION
A. Experiments
Proposed augmented triplet loss function has been tested
on 3-deers dataset and 9-humans dataset. From the video
data, 1400 images were sampled from a 6-minute video of
three animals, each shot separately. Among these, 1120 were
classiﬁed as training data and 280 as test data, and the image
size was resized to 32*32. For the human set, 9 different
people were ﬁlmed, and 600 images were sampled from each
of them. Fig. 4 shows a 2D representation of the training data
projected onto the embedding space by the t-SNE algorithm.
The center of each color-coded class is represented by a red
dot.
B. comparision
We compared the number of pairs generated during training
with loss functions such as HTL and Quadruplet Loss and
found that A TL generates fewer pairs. In Augmented triplet
loss, only |Ci+Cj|anchor pairs are needed for each violation
class pair. If there are a ﬁnite number of kviolating class pairs,
then the total number of anchor pairs generated is k(|Ci+Cj|).
Since both kand|Ci+Cj|are at most n, the number of
anchor pairs generated is O(n2). In this method, the size of
the feature vector in the embedded space is set to be 10. By
limiting the number of feature vectors to a small number, the
memory efﬁciency is improved. We applied our method to the
deer dataset and tested it. The results show that our method
can identify the deer with 99.64% accuracy after 10 training
trials. In the future, we would like to measure the relationship
between the number of training sessions and the accuracy by
measuring the execution speed and memory.
V. C ONCLUSION
We demonstrated the novel idea of recognizing individual
trouble making deer by proposing a new method, which we
termed Augmented Triplet Network in this paper. We found
that our method recognizes the individual deer with more
than 99% of accuracy. Due to the static nature of the cam-
era installed in various locations, the accuracy of individual
recognition is slightly penalized sometimes however this can
be increased in two ways. One can be done by increasingthe image and video quality of the camera. The other way
is by increasing the number of features to be included in
the classiﬁer. The paper presents a proposed Augmented
Triplet Network in comparison with the available methods
(hierarchical triplet loss and stochastic triplet loss and so) and
also evaluated the method in terms of deer. In the future work,
we plan to increase the datasets of deers and also upgrade the
proposed algorithm with other features in order to increase the
accuracy of recognition. Furthermore, we need to improve the
performance of the classiﬁer which can be done by increasing
the number of features. In our future works, we will apply
our methods to recognize other wild animals thereby helping
to protect the crops of farmers from further damage.
